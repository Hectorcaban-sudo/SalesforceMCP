A **Past Performance Agent** is a specialized AI entity designed to manage and leverage a company’s history of completed projects—specifically in the context of government contracting (GovCon) or large-scale B2B industries. Its primary goal is to prove to potential clients that "we’ve done this before, and we did it well."

In industries like defense or federal contracting, "Past Performance" is a formal metric used by evaluators to determine risk. Here is what an agent dedicated to this domain would typically do:

### 1. Data Aggregation and Normalization
The agent acts as a central brain for disparate data sources. It scrapes internal systems (like Salesforce, Deltek, or SharePoint) to find:
* **Contract Details:** Award amounts, dates, and NAICS codes.
* **Performance Metrics:** Whether the project was on time and within budget.
* **CPARS Integration:** Automatically pulling and indexing *Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System* (CPARS) ratings to identify high-scoring projects.

### 2. Intelligent Search and Retrieval (RAG)
When a new Request for Proposal (RFP) is released, the agent performs a semantic search to find the most relevant historical matches. Instead of just searching for "software," it can distinguish between "cloud migration for a legacy mainframe" and "front-end web development," ensuring the best technical fit is surfaced.

### 3. Automated Drafting of Narratives
One of the most time-consuming tasks in proposal writing is tailoring past performance stories to fit specific RFP requirements. The agent can:
* Synthesize technical data into a cohesive **"Success Story."**
* Rewrite existing project descriptions to emphasize specific keywords found in a new solicitation.
* Ensure that the "Value Added" section of a past performance sheet aligns with the current client’s pain points.

### 4. Gap Analysis and Risk Mitigation
The agent evaluates the company’s portfolio against a target contract to identify weaknesses:
* **The "Gap" Check:** "We are bidding on a \$50M project, but our largest past performance is only \$10M."
* **The "Risk" Alert:** Identifying if a project had a "Marginal" rating in a specific area (like Quality or Schedule) so the capture team can address it proactively in the proposal.

### 5. Compliance and Cross-Referencing
The agent ensures that the "Past Performance" volume of a bid meets strict government formatting rules, such as:
* Verifying that points of contact (POCs) for past clients are still active and accurate.
* Checking that the project dates fall within the "Recency" window (typically the last 3–5 years).

---

### Why it Matters
For a government contractor, a Past Performance Agent transforms static archives into a **predictive asset**. It moves the company away from "What projects do we have?" to "Which of our past successes guarantees we win this specific bid?"
